About me

Pickleball is a racket sport that is similar in premise to tennis. With smaller courts and more rounds, it is easier to get into, whilst still maintaining a high skill ceiling. People of all ages love to play the game, and can excel at it!

Where can you play?

Anywhere you find a park you will probably find a pickleball court! With pickleball being the fastest growing sport in the nation, and being vary similar to tennis, new courts are popping up all over.

On rainy or cold days, you can often find inside courts as well!

When can you play?

Pickleball is a fun sport to play any time of the year. Too cold to be outside? Try an indoor court! To hot for the summer? Many courts stay open late!

Why should you play?

Honestly, why not? Most of the courts are free, so the equipment needed is relatively cheap. It is easy to pick up and only as intense as you want it to be, which is a nice advantage over tennis. With quick games you can stay for a while or only 20 minutes. Give it a try!

How do you play?

I will not go into extended detail on all of the rules here. In its simplest form, all you need to do to play is hit the ball over the net. If you hit the ball out of the court, or the ball bounces twice on your side, the other team gets a point. However, only the team serving can score, just like in volleyball.

To serve, just hit the ball from outside of your box to the box that is diagonal to you (depicted above). Remember, underhand hits only on the serve.

Last but not least, if you are standing in the kitchen, you cannot hit the ball out of the air. The ball must bounce at least once before you can hit it.
